4 Final Notes & Other References

Section 2 touched on creating 2D images from functions. For this as a method unto itself, look at Functional Images from Conal Elliott. More commonly, though, this method is used as a way of creating "solid textures" which are used in conjunctions with 3D models. See Ken Perlin's seminal paper, An image synthesizer, for a starting point on this. Beyond this, there are countless references on how shaders are used in 3D graphics - both in realtime applications such as games (for which OpenGL's shaders are used extensively), and in computer-generated imagery for movies (such as RenderMan Shading Language).

I mentioned that the distance functions in section 3 were a sort of extension of this - particularly, of what we can do with functions that map points in space to something (color, or a distance bound, for instance). Another application of this same principle can be found in the Hypertexture approach of Perlin and Hoffert - in which a function maps points in 3D space to density at that point, rather than minimum-distance.

Íñigo Quílez is one of the leading figures on sphere tracing over the past 10 years or so, and has done extensive work in implementing, explaining, and popularizing them. His website is full of articles on the mathematics and implementation (start at Raymarching Distance Fields) and his Shadertoy profile is full of excellent examples (and this web application's sphere tracer is based on one such example).

The standard paper giving the mathematics behind sphere tracers is Numerical Methods for Ray Tracing Implicitly Defined Surfaces by John C. Hart. Many other optimizations have been done on top of this basic framework; e.g. see the paper Enhanced Sphere Tracing. I also made use of:

  • From Gábor Liktor: Ray tracing implicit surfaces on the GPU
  • From Morgan McGuire: Numerical Methods for Ray Tracing Implicitly Defined Surfaces
  • Fabrice Castel's blog:
    • Sphere Tracing 101 - Hello Sphere!
    • Sphere Tracing 102 - Simple Lighting
    • Sphere Tracing 103 - Generating a Cube
    • Sphere Tracing 104 - CSG
    • Sphere Tracing 105 - Domain Repetition
  • The Book of Shaders

The nature of fractals makes them difficult to handle in many other rendering techniques, and sphere tracing is often used here. See the Syntopia blog from Mikael Hvidtfeldt Christensen and the older paper Ray Tracing Deterministic 3-D Fractals.

Fragmentarium and Shadertoy provide similar a framework to the WebGL environment of this web application, but many more features.
